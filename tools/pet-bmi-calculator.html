Body Condition Assessment

Understanding Body Condition Score (BCS)

Veterinarians use a 1-9 scale Body Condition Score to assess pet weight:

BCSCategorySigns
1-3UnderweightRibs, spine, hip bones easily visible; no fat cover
4-5IdealRibs easily felt; visible waist from above; abdominal tuck from side
6-7OverweightRibs felt with pressure; waist barely visible; slight belly
8-9ObeseRibs not felt; no waist; distended abdomen; fat deposits

Why Weight Matters

  • Overweight pets have 2.5 years shorter lifespan on average
  • Excess weight increases risk of diabetes, arthritis, and heart disease
  • Even 10-15% above ideal weight is clinically significant

What To Do

  • Underweight: Increase food by 10-20% and consult your vet to rule out health issues
  • Ideal: Maintain current feeding — you're doing great!
  • Overweight: Reduce food by 10-15%, increase exercise gradually
  • Obese: Consult your vet for a weight loss plan — never crash-diet a pet
